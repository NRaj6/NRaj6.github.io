Rise Against Hunger Learning Survey (HackNC 2020)

I worked with a team of four wonderful women from my school to build an web app prototype for Rise Against Hunger, a local non-profit battling food insecurity. The app would be a WordPress-compatible interactive quiz on various facts about the scope of world hunger, with a contact form at the end. For my part, I coded a JavaScript version of the quiz to be integrated with our Figma mockups, which provided the user with various resources depending on their choice of leanring path, as well as experimented with PHP to help create a Wordpress plugin. Our prototype won both the HackNC 2020 Nonprofit Challenge and placed in the top 25 overall to win the Wolfram Award.

Byte's School Survival Hacks (TechTogether New York 2020)

This circuit, given a sound file in .wav format, outputs the audio in an audible form. This is accomplished by series-connecting a capacitor to the speaker, which "cleans up" the sound signal by filtering out interference from its DC component, leaving only the AC component to be blasted by the speaker.

Personal Website

This circuit could serve as a high, low, or band-pass filter. High-pass filters only pass frequencies above a certain threshold in a given signal; low-pass filters only below a threshold; and band-pass between two specified cutoff frequencies. These filters are often used in audio systems to amplify or purify certain sounds. In this circuit, I used an op-amp as well as several resistors and capacitors. In the image above, the circuit is in band-pass form; however, with a simple rearrangment of some of the jumper wires, a high- or low-pass filter can be constructed as well.
